## What this site is

A directory of ambulance providers in India, compiled from:

- Verification phone calls placed by maintainers and contributors.
- Public-domain government data (e.g., NHM Karnataka, BBMP listings,
  RTI responses).
- Publicly listed phone numbers and addresses from provider websites,
  hospital websites, and public business directories.
- OpenStreetMap and other open-data sources, cross-referenced.

Every record carries a `last_verified_at` date and a freshness badge.
Records older than 30 days are marked stale (amber). Records older than
60 days are hidden from the public map until re-verified.

1. **Call 108 (or your state EMS number) first in any emergency.** Do
   not use this directory as a substitute for emergency services.

## User-submitted reports

The site accepts reports from members of the public about their actual call experiences with listed providers. These reports are:

- **Submitted voluntarily** by patients, families, doctors, or other contributors after a real call.
- **Moderated** before any publication. Pending reports live as GitHub issues until a maintainer reviews.
- **Aggregated** into per-provider statistics (answer rate, median response time, median fare) once published.
- **NOT real-time availability.** The "what users report" section on each provider page is *historical observation*, never a guarantee that a vehicle is available right now.
- **Disputable.** Providers may submit a dispute via the takedown process; we annotate the contested report and may remove it if found unverifiable or in bad faith.
- **Licensed under CC BY-NC-SA 4.0** by the contributor upon submission.

We do not verify each report against the provider's internal records — we cannot. We do screen for plausibility (a fare of ₹999,999 is implausible; a date in the future is impossible) and for bad-faith submissions (mass identical text, coordinated attacks on a single provider). Honest, contradictory reports are kept and aggregated; readers see the dispersion.

- We do not assert motive or wrongdoing in affiliation edges. We
  publish factual edges ("Hospital X's published ER number routes
  calls to Provider Y, observed on [date]") with sources cited.

## Health and safety information

If you are reading this in an emergency:

> **Dial 108.** Karnataka, and most Indian states, operate the 108
> Arogya Kavacha / state EMS line free of charge. They will dispatch
> a vehicle and route you to the nearest appropriate facility.

This site is for non-emergency planning, transfer arrangement,
research, and informed comparison — not for in-the-moment dispatch.
