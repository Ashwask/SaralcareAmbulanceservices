**Subject:** Request for information regarding call volume and response-time performance of the Arogya Kavacha — 108 Emergency Medical Services (EMS) in the State of Karnataka, for the most recent twelve-month period.

Sir/Madam,

I am applying under the Right to Information Act, 2005 for the following information related to the Arogya Kavacha — 108 EMS, operated by the Government of Karnataka under the National Health Mission. The information is requested for purposes of civic research and the maintenance of a public-good, openly licensed directory of emergency medical services in India.

(Reference: prior RTI applications dated _______ and _______.)

The information sought is as follows:

1. **Call volume.** For the most recent twelve (12) months for which data is available:
   - Total number of calls received by the 108 control centre in Karnataka.
   - Monthly breakdown.
   - District-wise breakdown.
   - Breakdown by call category (medical emergency, accident, maternal/neonatal, transfer, hoax, dropped, other).

2. **Response time.** For the same twelve-month period:
   - Average response time (minutes between call assignment and on-scene arrival) by district.
   - Median response time by district.
   - Number and percentage of calls with response time exceeding 30 minutes, by district.
   - Number and percentage of calls where no vehicle was dispatched, with reasons.

3. **Service availability.** For the same twelve-month period:
   - Number of station-hours where any 108 ambulance was operationally unavailable, by station and month.
   - Reasons for unavailability (vehicle breakdown, crew unavailability, etc.) in summary form.

4. **Hospital handover.** For the same twelve-month period:
   - Top 20 hospitals in Karnataka by number of 108 patient handovers received.
   - For Bengaluru Urban specifically, the full list of hospitals receiving 108 patient handovers, with counts.

5. **Complaints.** Total number of complaints received by the 108 service or by NHM Karnataka about the 108 service in the twelve-month period, with summary of categories (delayed response, vehicle condition, staff conduct, billing, other).
