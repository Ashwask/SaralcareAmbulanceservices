**Subject:** Request for information regarding the Arogya Kavacha — 108 Emergency Medical Services (EMS) in the State of Karnataka.

Sir/Madam,

I am applying under the Right to Information Act, 2005 for the following information related to the Arogya Kavacha — 108 EMS, operated by the Government of Karnataka under the National Health Mission. The information is requested for purposes of civic research and the maintenance of a public-good, openly licensed directory of emergency medical services in India.

The information sought is as follows:

1. **Station list.** A complete list, as on the date this application is processed, of all 108 Arogya Kavacha ambulance stations operating within the State of Karnataka, with the following columns for each station:
   - District, taluk, and locality of the station.
   - Postal address.
   - Year of commissioning.
   - Operating contractor (i.e., GVK EMRI, Ziqitza Healthcare Limited (ZHL), BVG-UKSAS, or any other).
   - Date of last operational audit by the State.
   - Date of last contract renewal.

2. **Contractor-wise allocation.** A mapping of districts in Karnataka to the contractor operating 108 services in that district, as on the date this application is processed.

3. **State-wide totals.** The total number of 108 ambulance stations in Karnataka as on the date this application is processed, with a breakdown by:
   - Urban / rural classification.
   - Contractor (GVK EMRI, ZHL, BVG-UKSAS, others).

I undertake to pay any reasonable fee prescribed under the Right to Information (Regulation of Fee and Cost) Rules. Please indicate the fee payable for the requested information and the mode of payment.

If any part of the requested information is held by an authority other than your office, kindly transfer the relevant part under Section 6(3) of the Act and inform me of the same.

If you are of the view that any part of this information should be denied on grounds available under Sections 8 or 9 of the Act, please furnish reasons in writing, citing the specific clause invoked, so that I may, if so advised, prefer an appeal.

**Filing notes (internal — do not include with filed application):**

- File this in person at the NHM office at Vikasa Soudha or send by registered post with acknowledgement. Personal filing recommended for tracking.
- Keep a signed copy of the application and the postal acknowledgement.
- Statutory response window: 30 days from receipt by the PIO. Acknowledgement within 5 working days.
- If no response in 30 days, file first appeal with the First Appellate Authority (likely the Director of Health Services or the Mission Director, NHM Karnataka).
