# Grants tracker

A public log of grant applications we've considered, applied for, won, and
lost. Updated whenever the status changes.

## Status legend

- **Identified** — programme is a fit but we haven't applied yet (often
  blocked on entity registration).
- **Applied** — submission filed; date logged.
- **Awarded** — funds received; amount + scope logged.
- **Declined** — not selected; the reason (when given) is logged so future
  applications can adjust.
- **Withdrawn** — we pulled the application (mission drift, conflict, etc.).

## Process notes for future applicants (us, next time)

- **Lead time**: most foundation grants have 4-8 week review cycles after a 2-4 week proposal-writing period. Plan 3 months end-to-end.
- **Entity requirement**: every grant above expects either a registered NPO or a fiscal sponsor. Wikimedia and Mozilla accept fiscal sponsors (e.g., Code for India, Aspiration). That can be a v1.5 path before Section 8 is filed.
- **Reporting**: most require quarterly reports on spend + outcomes. We'd publish those to `finance/` in the repo.
- **Conflict-of-interest**: each application surfaces our commercial-licensing option as a potential conflict. Disclose proactively; declare we don't grant commercial licences to grant funders or their affiliates during the grant period.

## Why this file exists publicly

Most open civic projects keep funding conversations private. We don't —
because the same forces (which-providers-stay-listed, what-edges-get-published)
that we hold ourselves to on the dataset apply to how we get funded. Public
funding history = no surprises later.

When the first grant is applied for, the application narrative itself will
land here as a Markdown file, so future applicants can reuse what worked.

## Crowd-funding considerations

Not currently active. Would need:
- Section 8 entity to receive funds.
- A monthly burn rate to justify (we don't have one yet).
- A "what we're solving this quarter" surface to make the ask concrete.

Until those exist, we point sponsors at `/donate` which explains why we're
not yet taking money.
